Our Story

About June Jam

Since 1978, June Jam has been Delaware's premier outdoor concert experience — a slice of peace, love, and rock ’n’ roll right in the heart of the First State.

What started as a small gathering of music lovers has grown into a beloved tradition. Every summer, thousands of fans gather for a weekend of live performances, on-site camping, community, and good times.

Rooted in the Delaware countryside, June Jam is more than a concert. It's a homecoming — the kind of place where strangers become friends and the music never really stops.

Camping FAQ

We recommend bringing a tent, sleeping bag, camping chair, flashlight, toiletries, and any personal items. Don't forget sunscreen, comfortable shoes, and a reusable water bottle. For RV camping, bring your own hookup cables and leveling blocks.

Yes, free hot showers are available for all campers. They're located in the central facilities building near the general camping area. Peak hours are 7-9am, so plan accordingly for shorter lines.

Yes, camping wristbands allow re-entry to the campgrounds 24/7. However, vehicle access is restricted during event hours (10am-2am). If you need to move your vehicle during these times, please see a camping attendant for assistance.

Yes, we have a camping store with basic supplies, snacks, and ice. Food vendors are also located at the festival entrance, just a short walk from the campgrounds. Breakfast items are available from 7-10am daily.

Families are welcome! Children under 12 camp for free with a ticket-holding adult. We recommend family camping in the designated quiet zone (sector D). All minors must be accompanied by an adult at all times in camping areas.

Vendors

June Jam's vendor marketplace is a beloved part of the festival experience. We welcome food vendors, artisans, craftspeople, and merchandise sellers to join us for the weekend.

Space is limited and allocated first-come, first-served. Applications open each spring.
